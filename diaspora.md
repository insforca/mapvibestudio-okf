# MapVibe Studio — Diaspora and Expat Audience

## Primary audience

Latin American diaspora in the United States. Documented buyer communities include:

- **Venezuelans** — cities: Caracas, Maracaibo, Valencia, Barquisimeto, Mérida
- **Brazilians** — cities: São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Florianópolis, Belo Horizonte, Salvador, Curitiba, Porto Alegre
- **Colombians** — cities: Bogotá, Medellín, Cartagena, Cali
- **Cubans** — cities: La Habana, Santiago de Cuba, Trinidad, Cienfuegos, Varadero
- **Mexicans** — cities: Ciudad de México, Guadalajara, Monterrey, Puebla, Tijuana, Cancún, Oaxaca
- **Central Americans** — any city in Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Costa Rica, Panama, Nicaragua
- **South Americans** — any city in Peru, Argentina, Ecuador, Chile, Bolivia, Uruguay, Paraguay
- **Dominican Americans** — cities: Santo Domingo, Santiago de los Caballeros
- **Puerto Ricans in the US** — cities: San Juan, Ponce, Bayamón

## Secondary audiences

- Study abroad students and alumni
- Anyone with a meaningful personal connection to a city outside their current country of residence
- Housewarming gift buyers for friends or family who recently moved

## Cultural context

The emotional driver for diaspora buyers is the connection to a specific hometown or city abroad. This is often described as nostalgia, añoranza (Spanish), or saudade (Portuguese for Brazilian expats). The product addresses this by placing the buyer's chosen city at the center of a wall art piece.

MapVibe Studio's positioning is diaspora-first: the brand, content, and product positioning are built around immigrants and expats, not the general wall art market.

## City coverage

The map editor supports any city, town, village, or country worldwide. Buyers from any diaspora community can order a map of their specific hometown — not just the major cities.
